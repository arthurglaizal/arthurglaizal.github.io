Research


Usability testing

First, I tested the interface to reveal existing problems.


Responsive design

The first thing I noticed is that there is a mobile version and a desktop version, at two different web addresses.

Unfortunately, all pages don‚Äôt automatically detect your device.

Consequently, there is a high risk for a mobile user to land on a desktop/large screen design layout.



Other problems

In the mobile version, I rapidly noticed many design problems. First, the lack of visual consistency. For example, the two sides panels (navigation drawer/side sheet) do not match visually: colors, icons, typography.



With more time I would have tested the site arborescence and the needs with Audiofanzine users. To do so, the metrics from ‚Äúm.audiofanzine‚Äù would have been a good insight.


Competitive analysis

I also looked at the competitors to see the classic interfaces and pick ideas. Among other things, the tab bar used in all the apps is more accessible compare to the mobile site navigation bar.



IDEATE AND TEST


Sketch

Therefore, to improve the experience, I had 3 objectives in mind for my design:

  • Clarify information architecture.
  • Create the app identity with a uniform User Interface.
  • Allow the user to personalize his news/ads feeds.

Test

Following up with iterative usability testing allowed me to confirm design decisions.



Mockups and charter

The graphic charter includes a new logo to better match the new aesthetic.


In terms of navigation, the tab bar allows you to move between the different sections of the site: News, Ads, Favorite, Message/Forum, Profile. As soon as he arrives, the user can scroll through the site‚Äôs news feed in a pleasant way. The same goes for the ads.


He can also access specific content via the search bar: Stratocaster Fender guitar ads, forum on a drum machine, etc.
