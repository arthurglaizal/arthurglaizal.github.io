VR Lightping

Brief

University project for the development of virtual reality (VR) in the Industrial Design pole of the UTC (University of Technology of Compi√®gne) : Help a student duo in the realization of their virtual prototype.

Virtual reality has many advantages for prototyping. Flexibility of scale, color, shape and environment.

I helped with the presentation of the Lightping product imagined by Yanna Carli and Jeremy Pointel for a pingpong table brand. This one is a night table lighting.

Virtual reality
  • 2019
  • 3 months
  • University project
  • Jeremy Pointel
  • Yanna Carli





Development

The solution was developed on Unity3D with Steam VR hardware. There are other software solutions including "Meshroom VR" created by a former student of the design department and which offers realistic turnkey renderings.

  • Global design and development of the application.
  • Realist materials (PBR).
  • The product can be carried in the RV simulation so that it can be observed from different points of view.





Final Product

A standalone virtual reality application. The user is in a night environment with the pingpong table and the "Lightping" solution. He can teleport around the table. The objects are at scale 1. This allows the user to see if the solution could interfere with the game.

The first video shows the inside view of the RV helmet.

The second demonstrates the scale correspondence between the RV and reality. In fact, when you place the ping pong table (here with the functional model of the project attached) so that it corresponds to its location in the helmet, you can achieve an accuracy of less than a cm.






In parallel, I developed a series of video tutorials and a project so that the students could be autonomous with the material.
