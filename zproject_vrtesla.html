VR UX Tesla Byton

Brief

To develop a virtual reality application allowing the customer to test several prototypes without going through physical prototypes in the transport sector.

The application is a technical demo based on non-confidential 3D and can also serve as a showcase for the agency.

Virtual Reality
  • 2019
  • 3 months
  • Agency work (internship)
  • Leap Motion
  • Steam VR
  • Unity 3D
  • UX


Research

The Tesla model S was released in 2017 and information about its interior is available on the internet. In particular, there is a web simulation of the UX of this screen. It was therefore easy to reproduce the UI and UX.

On the other hand, the M-Byte is an electric SUV concept car created by Byton whose marketing should start in 2020. Significant research work was necessary to identify all the interactions and reproduce the IU when necessary.

I have created a hierarchy of interactions according to their nature and depth in the "sitemap" of the application.






Development

The entire application was designed with Unity3D and Steam VR hardware.
  • 3D model integration.
  • User interface integration.
  • Creation of interactions in Unity. (Scripts or visual scripts)
  • Testing and debugging the program.



Final Product

A functional application presented to the customer for demonstration. In the interior, it is possible to interact with different car interfaces.

  • Simultaneous control of the application via a 2D interface on a touch screen computer.
  • Interactions with a physic pad.
  • Choice of vehicle.
  • Choice of interfaces; the interior of the vehicle.
  • Interactions with interfaces.

The application was then taken over by an engineer who integrated the client's confidential 3D into the application and continued to develop it.

For the customer, it is a cheaper and much more flexible method of testing designs to scale.






Arthur Glaizal (2020)
