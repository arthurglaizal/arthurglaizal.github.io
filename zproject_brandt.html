Alarm clock Brandt BCR173 v2.0

Brief

Proposal of a new version of the Brandt BCR173 alarm clock that meets more ergonomic criteria. These are not only anthropomorphic but take into account all parts of the object's use including the user experience.

Ergonomics
  • 2019
  • 4 months
  • University project
  • Marianne Kassubeck
  • Estelle Andr√©



Development

Applications of the different steps of an ergonomic analysis. This is largely based on the observation of usage. It is therefore very concrete and requires gathering users according to defined profiles. It is therefore possible to have a more precise picture of the actual use and compare it to the designer's prescribed use. Care must be taken with regard to the factors of variability: day/night, age, language, etc.

  • Determination of the relevant population to be tested.
  • Creation of different observation tools: observation grid, incident table, etc.
  • Interviews and observations.
  • 33 Models.
  • Analyse.
  • Proposal of a new design.




Final product

The ergonomic approach allows the designer to better understand the use of his product. A new product proposal that is more pleasant and efficient and based on experience is then possible.

We have designed a new navigation, a reorganization of the interface and a new design for the alarm clock.
