Eastpak / Flex pak

Brief

Building upon Eastpak's expertise in the luggage industry, this project aims to penetrate a market often characterized by outdated and feminine-associated products by offering an innovative model in terms of utility and image, targeted towards a young and diverse audience.

Industrial design
  • 2018
  • 4 months
  • University project
  • Laure Kraemer



Research

A thorough analysis of existing shopping carts and Eastpak products was conducted. Following an intensive search for concepts and usage scenarios, my partner and I developed an idea where the cart is convertible into a backpack. Given Eastpak's iconic product status and considering that young individuals hesitate to invest in seldom-used shopping carts, this solution seemed most relevant.




Technical development

The cart-backpack is designed to be convertible through an accordion system. A telescopic arm extends the fabric beneath the backpack to provide additional space.





Prototype

A scaled-down prototype was created to validate feasibility and present the concept.




Final Product

The Flexpak, a versatile bag. Convertible into a shopping cart, it enables students to carry their belongings while effortlessly grocery shopping.


Arthur Glaizal (2020)
